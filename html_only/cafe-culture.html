What is coffee culture?

When people think about coffee in Canada, they often think about ordering a double-double at Tim Hortons. But coffee culture is much more than stock-standard drip coffee. Ten years ago it would have been hard to find any semblance of coffee culture in cities like Toronto beyond the ubiquitous Starbucks, but things have now changed. Cities like Toronto and Montreal are festooned with unique, boutique cafe's serving up barista-styled coffee drinks, and possibly home-made patisserie and treats. Much of this stems from a need to look beyond fast-coffee, and the establishment of numerous in-house roasteries, developing their own coffee bean profiles. Coffee also transcends different societies, from Italian, to Turkish, to Lebanese, to Japanese. Coffee culture is about the coffee bean, but it is just as much about the person making the coffee, the quality of the ingredients, and even the texhnique used. Espresso machines, siphon coffee, Moka pot, V60, or Ibrik all produce differing renditions of coffee.
